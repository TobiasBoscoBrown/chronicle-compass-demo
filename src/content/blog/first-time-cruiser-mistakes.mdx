Cruising is one of the best values in travel, but it is also one of the easiest to get wrong if you book on a price-comparison site without thinking through the details.

## 1. Picking the cheapest cabin without reading the deck plan

An interior cabin is a great deal, unless it sits directly under the buffet or above the engine room. I always pull the deck plans before quoting.

## 2. Booking shore excursions through the ship

Ship-run excursions are convenient but often double the price of a local operator. For most ports I have a vetted local guide I can hand you off to.

## 3. Underestimating gratuities and drink packages

Daily gratuities add $15 to $25 per person per day, and a drink package can be the difference between an $1,800 cruise and a $2,800 one. We model both before you book.

## 4. Treating embarkation day as a vacation day

Fly in the night before. Always. A missed-port story is funny later, but it ruins the first 48 hours of your trip.

## 5. Skipping travel insurance

Cruise lines cancel itineraries more than you would think. A $200 policy can return five figures.
