Every spring my inbox fills with the same question: when should we go to Europe? The honest answer is almost never July. Here is the breakdown I share with clients, including the two shoulder windows I keep for my own travel.

## Late April to mid-June

Mild weather, gardens in bloom, restaurants not yet on summer schedules, and hotel rates still 20 to 30% below July peaks. The downside: a few destinations, the Greek Islands and parts of the Amalfi Coast, are not fully open until late May.

## September to mid-October

My personal favorite. The Mediterranean is still warm, the kids are back in school, and Tuscany is in harvest. Book hotels four to six months out. September fills up faster than summer with returning travelers.

## What to avoid

- The first two weeks of August, when locals close shops, prices peak, and the heat is brutal.
- Christmas week in Paris or Rome, beautiful, but every museum is mobbed.
- Easter week anywhere in Italy or Spain, when religious holidays shut more than you would think.

## How I'd plan it for you

Tell me roughly when you can travel and roughly where you want to go. I'll come back with two or three windows, the rate difference between them, and what's actually open in each. No pressure, no fee for the conversation.
